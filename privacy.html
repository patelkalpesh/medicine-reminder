💊 Medicine Reminder — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 21 June 2026

1. What Data We Collect

The Medicine Reminder app stores the following data that you enter:

  • Medicine names, dosages, schedules, and photos
  • Dose logs (taken/skipped/snoozed)
  • Health measurements (BP, heart rate)
  • Doctor/pharmacy contacts
  • Caretaker contacts
  • Appointment dates
  • Stock and cost information
  • App settings (PIN, language, notification preferences)

2. Where Data Is Stored

  • Locally: All data is stored in your browser's localStorage on your device. It never leaves your device unless you explicitly sync.
  • Google Drive (optional): If you sign in with Google, data is synced to your Google Drive's private appDataFolder. Only this app can access it. No other app or person can see your data.

3. Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do NOT have any server or database
  • We do NOT collect analytics or usage data
  • We do NOT share your data with anyone
  • We do NOT sell your data
  • We do NOT use cookies for tracking
  • We do NOT store your Google password

4. Google Drive Access

We request access to drive.appdata scope only. This allows the app to:

  • Create and read a single file in a hidden app-specific folder
  • This folder is invisible to you and other apps
  • We cannot access any of your other Google Drive files

5. Notifications

If you enable notifications, the app sends local browser notifications for medicine reminders. These are generated on your device — no data is sent to any server.

6. Data Deletion

You can delete all your data at any time by:

  • Clearing your browser's localStorage
  • Revoking Google Drive access from your Google Account settings
  • Uninstalling the PWA from your device

7. Security

  • PIN lock protects access to the app
  • Google OAuth 2.0 for secure authentication
  • HTTPS encryption for all data in transit
  • No server-side storage = no data breach risk

8. Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under 13. If used for a child's medication, a parent/guardian should manage the data.
